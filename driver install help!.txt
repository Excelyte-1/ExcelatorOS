Installing Drivers in ExcelatorOS
=================================

Installing drivers in ExcelatorOS is simple. Most drivers for **Linux Mint** or **Ubuntu** work perfectly, even though ExcelatorOS is technically a different OS.

──────────────────────────────────────────
Step 1: Identify Your Device
──────────────────────────────────────────
- Determine the hardware you need drivers for (graphics card, network adapter, etc.).  

──────────────────────────────────────────
Step 2: Find the Drivers
──────────────────────────────────────────
Here are links to the major GPU manufacturers’ drivers:  

**NVIDIA:**  
https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/drivers/  
*Find your GPU model and download the Linux driver.*

**Intel:**  
- Intel drivers can be tricky to locate.  
- A good tip: ask ChatGPT (or check Intel’s official support site) — it’s excellent for finding Linux drivers.

**AMD:**  
https://www.amd.com/en/support/download/drivers.html  
*Select your GPU and download the Linux driver.*

──────────────────────────────────────────
Step 3: Install the Drivers
──────────────────────────────────────────
- Follow the instructions provided on the manufacturer’s site.  
- Most of the time, installation involves downloading a file and running it, similar to Windows.

──────────────────────────────────────────
That’s it!
──────────────────────────────────────────
- Once the drivers are installed, your hardware should work perfectly with ExcelatorOS.  
- If you run into issues, refer to the manufacturers website for more information!
